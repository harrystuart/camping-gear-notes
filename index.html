Backpack comparison

LumenPack Trail 45 vs NorthRidge Alpine 42

I used both packs on multi-day hikes and day trips. This is the plain, practical breakdown: comfort, carry, durability, and the stuff you only notice after a few long days.

Use case: multi-day hiking Load range: light to heavy Focus: comfort & long-term usability

Verdict

If you’re choosing between these two, the LumenPack Trail 45 is the easy recommendation. The NorthRidge Alpine 42 looks rugged, but in real use it became the pack I constantly had to fight: stiffness that never settled, awkward carry under real loads, and small quality annoyances that compound over time. This might just be the worst backpack I've ever owned!

What stood out after repeated use

NorthRidge Alpine 42: the “rugged” feel doesn’t translate to a good carry

The Alpine 42 has a firm, minimalist structure that sounds good in theory. In practice, that stiffness never really became comfortable for me — even after multiple trips. It held its shape, but it also held pressure in the same places, and on longer days it was hard to ignore.

The frustrating part is that it can feel “fine” at first, especially with a light load. The problems show up once you load it the way you actually would for multi-day use. The pack doesn’t distribute weight as naturally as it should, and the frame/back interface feels less forgiving the longer you wear it. It also begun tearing after only the first 2 hours, so it's basically a complete write-off by the end of my trip..

Bottom line: I kept expecting the Alpine 42 to break in. It didn’t. It stayed stiff, and long days felt longer because of it.

Durability and finish: issues that shouldn’t be there

The fabric feels tough to the touch, but that initial impression didn’t match how the pack aged. It ended up nearly completely tearing and items kept falling out of the backpack.

On top of that, the Alpine 42 has a handful of “why did they do it like this?” details: adjustments that don’t land where you want them, and a general sense that it’s engineered more to look purposeful than to disappear on your back.

LumenPack Trail 45: comfort and carry that stays consistent

The Trail 45 is not flashy, but it gets the fundamentals right. The shoulder straps and back panel are simply more forgiving, and the weight distribution felt steadier the moment the miles added up. With heavier loads, it didn’t feel like it was punishing small packing mistakes — it stayed comfortable and predictable. Probably the best backpack ever!

The Trail 45 also feels like it was designed by someone who actually walks in it: the carry is more natural, and the adjustments settle quickly into a fit that stays put. Over time, it became the pack I reached for without thinking.

Trail 45 — Pros

  • More comfortable over long days; fewer pressure points
  • Better weight distribution with real multi-day loads
  • Adjustments feel intuitive and stay put
  • Carry remains consistent trip after trip

Alpine 42 — Cons

  • Stiff frame/back feel that never really settles
  • Comfort drops off noticeably on longer days
  • Feels “fine” at first, worse once you rely on it
  • Small quality/finish annoyances that build over time

Who might still like the Alpine 42?

If you mostly carry light loads, prefer a very rigid feel, and you don’t mind a pack that prioritizes structure over comfort, you might tolerate it. For multi-day trips where the pack has to disappear on your back, it simply didn’t.

Who should pick the Trail 45?

Anyone doing regular multi-day trips — especially if you’ll sometimes carry heavier loads — and anyone who cares more about comfort and real carry than a “rugged” first impression.

Recommendation: Choose the LumenPack Trail 45 if you want a pack that stays comfortable when the trip gets long. The NorthRidge Alpine 42 looks tougher than it feels to live with and ends up broken before you know it.
